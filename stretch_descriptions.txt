behind-the-back arm pull:Place both arms behind your back, holding your hands together by interlocking your fingers. Then, pull your arms down such that your chest pushes outward and upward.
wall-assisted arm pull:Placing your hand next to a wall, turn your body away from that arm such that your chest and arm make a 180 degree angle.
neck roll:Standing or sitting with your back straight up, roll your head in one direction slowly for several times
shoulder roll:Standing with your back straight up, steadily raise your shoulders and then roll them back down without bending your elbows
child's pose:Similar to a prostration pose, kneel with your knees tucked into your body and touching the ground. Straighten both arms and lay them on the ground above your head as you look down. Make sure you compress your body downward as much as possible
cow pose:While kneeling on the ground, place both hands in front and on the ground. Without bending your elbows, raise your bottom upward while keeping your upper body up, such that your lower spine bends in.
cobra pose:First, lie completely flat on the ground, face first. Then, placing both hands in the ground in front of your body, slowly lift up your head and upper body, bending your back upwards.
overhead stretch:(Triceps) Reach one arm in the air over your head, bend the elbow, and place the hand behind your back, hand flat. Keep the arm in place with the other arm by holding onto the elbow.
standing biceps stretch:(Biceps) With both arms behind your back, hold hands together by locking fingers, then place them onto you back. The higher the hands are on your back, the more the biceps are stretched.
wrist pull:Holding one arm out and forward, bring the other hand to grab the fingertips of the stretched hand, pulling them in.
wall press:Place both arms on a wall, relatively high up. Then, lower your body and head while keeping your spine straight. Move your feet back if necessary.
active floor stretch:In the cow position, move your arms to point in one direction (leftward or rightward).
seated-leg stretch:While seated, bring one leg up and turn it to be parallel to the ground. Rest it in that position on the other leg, and move your body forward and downward.
raised-leg stretch:While lying on the ground, curl one leg and bring in towards your body and chest.
standing quad stretch:(Quads) Standing up straight, bend one leg backward and hold it in place with one hand (preferably the corresponding hand).
lunges:(Quads) Standard lunges done in PE class
standing hamstring stretch:(Hamstrings) While standing, lift your leg up and rest it on a slightly elevated surface as you keep that leg straight.
tip-toe stretch:Seated, keep your feet on the ground and lift your legs up by holding your feet in the tip-toe position
wall-assisted foot stretch:Also while seated, put one foot against the wall and bend your leg inward.
cat-cow stretch:In the cow position, slowly bow your spine inward and outward.
